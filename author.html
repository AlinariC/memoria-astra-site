About the Author

The Voice Behind Memoria Astra

Alinari Campbell

Born under the misty skies of the Pacific Northwest, Alinari Campbell grew up captivated by the mysteries of ancient worlds, starlit futures, and the spaces between memory and imagination. A lifelong dreamer and storyteller, he found early inspiration in the works of Isaac Asimov, Ray Bradbury, and Ursula K. Le Guin — authors whose explorations of humanity’s hopes and sorrows left an indelible mark on his soul.

Alinari's professional life has been as versatile as his writing, blending technology, design, and a deep-rooted love of storytelling. As the founder of PixelPacific, he has helped craft digital experiences that bridge the worlds of art and science, believing that every line of code and every word written can shape reality itself. The Memoria Astra Cycle represents the culmination of a lifelong journey — an epic spanning breath, sorrow, rebirth, and transcendence across the stars. When not lost in writing or digital creation, Alinari enjoys road-tripping under vast skies, studying ancient myths, and sharing late-night conversations about existence and wonder with those who dream as he does.
